Data smoothing results in lower estimated volatility and lower correlations with other asset classes. Smoothing results when old or stale prices are used to estimate an assetâ€™s value or create an index. This, in turn, means higher risk-adjusted return measures and higher allocations to these asset classes.

In the case of smoothed prices, arbitrageurs are prevented from profiting for two main reasons. First, prices may not actually represent trading opportunities. Appraisals are estimates and do not represent actual purchase or selling prices. Second, even if prices represent true buy and sell values, transaction costs may be prohibitive and prevent arbitrage activities.
