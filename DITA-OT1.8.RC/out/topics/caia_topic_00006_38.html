The commodities topic has a relatively low weight on the exam and most of the material in this section is straightforward. Thus, there are some fairly easy points to be gained. In this topic, we consider commodities as potential investments; discuss theories of the forward curve; and describe convenience yields, the cost of carry, and the sources of return earned by commodity investors. We also discuss the role that commodity futures should play in well-diversified portfolios and various commodity investment strategies. Know why investors typically choose derivatives to gain access to commodities, rather than holding the physical commodities. Also, know the various directional and relative value commodity strategies. Finally, know how commodity indices have evolved and understand the factors that impact returns earned on commodity indices.
