LO 15.7

Appraisal-based indices rely on estimated property values derived from the sales comparison approach, the income approach, or the cost approach.

Advantages of appraisal-based models include no sample size issues and appraisals can be conducted frequently. Disadvantages of appraisal-based models include stale appraisal values, smoothing leading to underestimated volatility, and the need for current values for comparable properties.

The NCREIF Property Index (NPI) is a value-weighted, quarterly, appraisal-based index. The NCREIF collects data from members who are institutional real estate investment managers and maintains a large dataset of price and income data. The NPI, its sub-indices, and other indices are published from this data. The NPI return is an unleveraged, before-tax total return, which is the sum of the return from the income generated by the property and the return from the capital value of the property.

The comparable sales price approach values properties using data regarding the sales prices of comparable assets in the area are examined and used to value properties.

The discounted cash flow analysis method or income approach involves estimating net operating income over the life of a project and then discounting the cash flows to time zero at the required rate of return.
