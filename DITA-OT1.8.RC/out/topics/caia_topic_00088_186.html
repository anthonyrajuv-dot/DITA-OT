Foundational Financial Market Principles

LO 34.1: Demonstrate knowledge of three foundational principles of financial market regulation.

For example:

  • Discuss the three principles on which financial regulations are based.

Capital markets are based on trust, regardless of the idiosyncrasies of individual political jurisdictions. Political entities and governments provide enforcement and oversight of financial markets in order to encourage the following principles:

  1. Transparency.
  2. Market fairness and integrity.
  3. Government safeguarding of social and economic systems through rule of law.

Important points to keep in mind:

  • There are no global rules, so one must be aware and pay close attention to the rules in the applicable jurisdictions by verifying and checking.
  • Regulations are always changing, so this material is dated.
  • Regulatory interpretations are fluid.
  • Most regulatory schemes allow for combinations of civil, criminal, and monetary penalties against individuals and institutions that violate the law.
