Methods for pricing structured products include:

  • Partial differential equation (PDE) approach: assumes the value of a structured product can be determined by analyzing and predicting the movement of the underlying asset and then formulating a hedge based on the structured product and its underlying asset; uses dynamic hedging.
  • Simulation approach: hypothetical paths for the underlying assetâ€™s value are used to create an estimate for how the structured product would pay out under each situation.
  • Building blocks approach: breaks down a structured product into easier to analyze assets, estimates the value of each of the pieces, and then sums the values to determine the total value of product; uses static hedging.

Two principles to know about payoff diagrams:

  • Any payoff diagram shape can be constructed as long as there are adequate types and numbers of options with which to manage the risk exposure.
  • The level of the payoff diagram determines whether the product is priced appropriately.

Some studies suggest that structured products are often overpriced at issue. There is also evidence that total fees and expenses in some cases are lower than empirical studies suggest.
