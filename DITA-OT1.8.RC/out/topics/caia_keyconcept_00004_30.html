LO 1.7

The seven common components of an investment policy statement are:

  • Background.
  • Objective.
  • Asset classes.
  • Governance.
  • Manager selection.
  • Reporting and monitoring.
  • Strategic asset allocation.

Strategic asset allocation (SAA) attempts to meet the long-term asset allocation goals while optimizing risk and return. It also allows for the creation of a benchmark to track performance as well as implementing tactical asset allocation to capitalize on short-term market fluctuations. To start the SAA process, a macro view of the economy must be taken, as many asset classes are affected by macroeconomic conditions. All asset classes have an expected return consisting of the sum of three parts, as presented in the equation below:

asset class return = short-term real riskless rate + expected inflation + risk premium
