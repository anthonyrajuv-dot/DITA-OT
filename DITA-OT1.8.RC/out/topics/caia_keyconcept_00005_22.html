LO 16.7

Real estate derivatives can be used to hedge risk or to speculate on real estate. Advantages include: price discovery, improved risk management and ability to short residential real estate, lower transaction and management cost and higher diversification relative to direct property investment, and embedded leverage.

However, often liquidity is low and counterparty credit risk (in OTC contracts) remains a concern. Also, many emerging markets do not have indices to base derivatives on and the monthly marking-to-market creates accounting volatility. Further, high basis risk, as well as risk due to embedded leverage, remains a concern.
