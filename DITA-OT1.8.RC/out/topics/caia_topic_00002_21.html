LO 19.1

Relative value strategies attempt to predict changes in spreads between related prices or rates. Relative value strategies establish hedged positions with a long position on one security and a short position on a related security. Therefore, the strategy has very little market risk and tends to profit during normal market conditions when valuations converge to their equilibrium prices.

The classic relative value strategy trade identifies abnormal spreads between two related prices or rates and establishes a position that anticipates the convergence to normal levels.
