LO 16.4

There are four broad categories of hedge fund strategies based on systematic risk exposure:

  1. Equity strategies have some degree of systematic exposure. This strategy historically had the highest absolute value of correlation to equity markets.
  2. Event-driven and relative value strategies take positions based on some event that has occurred or is likely to occur. Relative value strategies (or convergent strategies) take positions where the valuation between two securities is expected to normalize. Historical returns are similar to the insurance industry with small profits for the majority of time periods with occasional large losses.
  3. Absolute return strategies attempt to earn positive returns regardless of the direction of the financial markets. Absolute return programs allow investors to achieve improved risk/return performance by capturing the benefits of fund diversification.
  4. Diversified strategies (global macro funds, systematic diversified funds, multistrategy funds, and FOFs) diversify across different investment themes. A FOF program combines a portfolio of individual hedge funds in an effort to diversify away idiosyncratic risk.
