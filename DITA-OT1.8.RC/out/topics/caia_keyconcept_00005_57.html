LO 22.5

The three sources of return for commodity futures contracts include:

  • Spot return is caused by movements in the spot commodity price in cash markets.
  • Collateral yield (or income return) is return on cash collateral, mainly from risk-free government instruments.
  • Roll yield is return from “rolling” commodity futures contracts over at maturity. Roll yield is positive in backwardated markets, and negative in contangoed markets.

Scarcity is difficult to observe, but could be implied from a downward sloping forward curve in a backwardated market, where investors place a premium value on owning the commodity in the spot market.
