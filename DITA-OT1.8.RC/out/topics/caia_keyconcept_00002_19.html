LO 18.4

  • Distressed debt hedge funds invest in securities of corporations in bankruptcy or corporations with a high risk of bankruptcy.
  • A naked call option position occurs when an investor is short a call option and does not own the underlying asset.
  • The challenge for the distressed hedge fund manager is to estimate the probabilities of various outcomes related to the recovery rate and recovery value.
  • Activist investors in distressed securities attempt to influence the recovery value and the timing of the exit from the bankruptcy process.
  • Capital structure arbitrage refers to holding a long position in relatively undervalued securities (senior debt or preferred stock) and a short position in relatively overpriced securities (junior debt or common stock), resulting in a hedged position.
  • If a distressed hedge fund believes a distressed firm is substantially undervalued, they may try to establish a controlling position in the firm during the bankruptcy process.
  • The cross-sectional average returns of distressed hedge funds reveal excellent Sharpe ratios from high returns and low volatility.
