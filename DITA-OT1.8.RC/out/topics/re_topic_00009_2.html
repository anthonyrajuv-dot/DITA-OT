Finding the Property

  1. CONSIDERATIONS
    1. Three considerations
      1. Location
      2. Price range—supply and demand
      3. Condition
    2. Location
      1. Buy-fix-sell property can be
        1. any type (such as residential, multifamily, office, retail, or industrial) and found in any market (high-end, low-end, or in-between); and
        2. any location (such as urban, suburban, or rural).
          1. Older housing stock tends to create more buy-fix-sell opportunities—these properties also may require more renovation
          2. Central cities, first- and second-ring suburbs, older mature housing stock
          3. Density—leads to more opportunities
          4. Wide range of values and conditions
          5. Changing neighborhoods—rising values
          6. Market activity
          7. Other buy-fix-sell activity
    3. Price range—supply and demand
      1. The next factor to consider is the price at which your finished buy-fix-sell property will sell.
        1. The market, not your renovation costs, will determine the property’s value.
      2. Supply and demand
        1. What price range in your market exhibits the highest demand?
        2. There is usually more demand for affordable housing.
        3. Competition
          1. Number of properties in your price range
          2. New construction in your price range
        4. Median price
          1. The halfway price point in the marketplace
          2. Average price
      3. Your knowledge of the demands of a particular price range
        1. Higher-priced homes tend to demand a higher level of interior design, fit, finish, and amenities.
        2. Lower-priced homes tend to have more generic finishes.
    4. Condition
      1. There are four types of condition: good, cosmetics-only, minor renovation, and major renovation.
        1. Good condition
          1. Buy, do little or nothing, and resell
          2. May need only to be cleaned
          3. Usually found as the result of being in the right place at the right time and of a successful marketing strategy for sourcing buy-fix-sell property
          4. Relatively rare occurrence
          5. Needs little work, if any
          6. Simply a property priced well under market value, usually because of a motivated seller (short sale, quick sale, estate settlement, etc.)
        2. Cosmetics-only
          1. This type of buy-fix-sell property probably holds the most potential for big profits.
          2. The work can be done relatively quickly and inexpensively, and redecorating results in the highest payoff.
          3. Needs only cosmetics is the primary market for buy-fix-sell, so expect competition.
          4. Cosmetics essentially means buy-redecorate-sell.
          5. Typical cosmetic improvements include the following:
            1. Clean out and clean up
            2. Interior patch and paint
            3. Carpet
            4. Linoleum
            5. Refinishing of wood floors
            6. New light fixtures—re-device
            7. Electrical cover plates
            8. Update kitchen
              • Appliances and range hood
              • Cabinets (reuse, refinish, replace)
              • Countertop
              • Sink
              • Flooring
              • Light fixtures
          6. Update bath
            1. Sink and vanity
            2. Faucets (sink, tub, shower, WC valve)
            3. Toilet
            4. Refinish or replace tub
            5. Tub surround
            6. Medicine cabinet, accessories
            7. Fan
          7. Light landscaping
            1. Pruning
            2. Weeding
            3. Planting
            4. Woodchips
          8. Minor odors
          9. New storm door (only if a standard size)
          10. New house numbers and mailbox
