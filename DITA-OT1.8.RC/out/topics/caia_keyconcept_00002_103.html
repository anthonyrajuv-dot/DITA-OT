LO 32.5

The traditional approach to asset allocation involves:

  • Strategic asset allocation decisions. These are top-level, long-term target allocations for the portfolio and are based on the risk/return preferences and constraints of the investor.
  • Tactical asset allocation decisions. These are the decisions that active managers make to capitalize on opportunities identified in the market.
  • Selecting managers and/or securities with superior risk-adjusted returns.

The new investment model is more flexible than the traditional model; alpha is sought independent of beta. Alternative asset classes may be used in the new model.

Actively managed portfolios contain active risks and active returns. Active risks are the risks that arise when a manager makes investment decisions designed to beat the returns of a specified benchmark. Active return is the return that results when a manager makes investment decisions designed to earn returns in excess of a passively managed benchmark.

Traditional asset allocation approaches include using:

  • Passive management products.
  • Enhanced index products.
  • Long-only active products.

Alpha is a zero-sum game if the following assumptions hold:

  • Identical investment horizons.
  • Identical risk tolerances.
  • No market segmentation.
  • Homogeneous expectations about risk and return.
  • Zero taxes or equal taxes for all.
  • Zero transactions costs.
  • These assumptions do not hold in the real world; therefore, alpha may not be a zero-sum game.
