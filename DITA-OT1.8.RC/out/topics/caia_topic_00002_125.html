  • Identify evidence for and organization of a due diligence process.

Because approximately 50% of hedge fund failures are due to operational risk, it is imperative that investors carefully conduct due diligence on potential managers. In addition, proper due diligence ensures that the investorâ€™s objectives (e.g., risk, return) and constraints (e.g., legal and tax) are able to be met by the fund. Due diligence is a disciplined approach for finding the best possible fund manager. The due diligence process consists of seven phases:

  1. Structural review.
  2. Strategic review.
  3. Administrative review.
  4. Performance review.
  5. Risk review.
  6. Legal review.
  7. Reference review.

The purpose of the remainder of this topic review is to discuss the key factors an investor should look for in each of the seven phases of the due diligence process.
