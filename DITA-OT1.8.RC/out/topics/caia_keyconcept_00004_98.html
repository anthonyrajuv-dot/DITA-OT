LO 11.3

Building a private equity portfolio can take one of the three forms: bottom-up, top-down, or mixed approach:

  • The bottom-up approach looks at all investment opportunities within specific, targeted PE markets in order to find the best fund managers.
  • The top-down approach starts with a macro level view of the economy, then assesses its impact on targeted PE markets, and finally determines the optimal combination (and weighting) of countries, industry sectors, and fund styles in order to best meet desired program objectives.
  • The mixed approach combines both methods.

Regardless of approach, investors must actively monitor markets and remain proactive.
