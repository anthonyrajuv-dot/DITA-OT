LO 23.2

The businesses in which venture capitalists invest are startup companies. A venture capital investor typically buys the companies’ convertible preferred stock and takes an active role in advising the companies.

Components of a startup’s business plan include explicit statement of business strategy, identification of the needs filled by the company’s products/services, and a list of resources needed to create and sell the products/services. The business plan must be internally consistent, logical, and comprehensive, in addition to being realistic.

Proper exit strategies are critical to ensure the eventual realization of value. Venture capitalists typically wish to be compensated in convertible preferred stock. Investments in venture capital have a potential payoff profile similar to a long call option as both have limited downside (i.e., cost of venture capital investment/option premium), unlimited upside, large positive skew, and leptokurtosis.
