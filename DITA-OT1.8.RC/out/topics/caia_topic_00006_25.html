Topic 4.4: Private Equity Operational Due Diligence

Operational Risk

Operational risk is defined as a series of risks (financial, reputational, etc.) that do not relate purely to investments. Operational due diligence (ODD) represents the process of evaluating this risk. Figure 4 illustrates the main areas that are reviewed as part of the ODD process.

Key Benefits of ODD

Performing ODD on private equity provides five critical benefits to investors:

  1. Gaining an understanding of how a private equity fund operates from a process and procedures perspective.
  2. Understanding how capable a firm is of adapting to outside changes such as new regulations or accounting pronouncements.
  3. Accessing valuable information on management itself and the risk modeling techniques it uses.
  4. Gaining a better understanding of how the fund works across the risk areas described in Figure 4, as well as assessing the benefits to LPs.
  5. Evaluating the capacity that the fund has to grow from a personnel and systems perspective.

Evaluating Meta Risk

A meta risk is a qualitative risk that is not captured by specific, measurable financial risks. Meta risks include risks related to:

  • Organizational and human behavior.
  • Moral hazards.
  • The misuse and over-reliance on quantitative tools.
  • The challenges of market interaction complexity.
  • The frequency and impact of extreme events in capital markets, which go beyond what is predicted in standard models.
