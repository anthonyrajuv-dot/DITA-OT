LO 21.4

Investors who acquire or license patents do so because they gain exposure to R&D without bearing the operational risks associated with buying the stock of companies making R&D investments. Buyers invest in patents for the following reasons:

  • Commercial purposes.
  • To trade the patent for other technologies or benefits.
  • Strategic uses.
  • Monetary exploitation.

If a patent is being infringed upon, it is usually more cost-effective to approach the user of the technology to negotiate a license agreement. If patent infringement is litigated, approximately 80% of the cases are resolved through settlements rather than trials.

Securitizations and mezzanine IP lending are common lending strategies that use patents as collateral.

There are several risks associated with investing in patents. They include illiquidity, operational/technological risks, obsolescence risk, expiration risk, macroeconomic/sector risks, regulatory risk, and legal risks.
