LO 3.1

An endowment refers to a large capital base that is maintained on an inflation-adjusted basis in perpetuity to generate annual income to help fund operational costs for various organizations.

A foundation is a nonprofit fund established for charitable purposes to support specific types of activities. In the United States, foundations have an annual minimum spending rate of 5%. In contrast, endowments have more flexibility in their spending. The four primary types of foundations include operating, community, corporate, and individual.
