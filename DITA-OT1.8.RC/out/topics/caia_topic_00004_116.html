Private Equity Benchmarks

LO 8.1: Demonstrate knowledge of the role of benchmarks in private equity investing.

For example:

  • Recognize needs served by investment benchmarks.
  • Identify steps of constructing a private equity benchmark.

A benchmark fulfills the following needs for private equity investors and managers:

  • As a point of reference for evaluating the performance of an investment manager or an individual investment.
  • Quantifies risk and return measures that are used as inputs in the strategic asset allocation decision process to create and maintain an optimized portfolio.
  • Historical outperformance of the benchmark by a PE manager can be used by the manager when marketing to new investors and in communications to current investors.
  • To assess how PE managers achieve their historical performance record, if flaws exist in the PE manager’s investment process, and if manager incentives are properly structured.

For a private equity (PE) fund, benchmarking is usually tied to the individual fund manager and focuses on how the manager performed in the past and how they are doing currently.

The steps for using a suitable PE benchmark are as follows:

Step 1: Determine the current value of a PE asset and its historical performance.

Step 2: Select a suitable benchmark.

Step 3: Examine the performance differential between asset and benchmark.
