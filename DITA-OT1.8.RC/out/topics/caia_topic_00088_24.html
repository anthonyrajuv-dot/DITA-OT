Using Attributes to Classify Real Estate

LO 16.2: Demonstrate knowledge of eight attributes used to differentiate the NCREIF real estate investment styles.

For example:

  • Describe individual properties according to the three NCREIF real estate investment styles and the eight attributes developed by NCREIF.
  • Describe real estate portfolios according to the three NCREIF real estate investment styles.

Style analysis is not just applied to individual properties but can also be applied to property managers and portfolios of properties. This means that a core real estate manager would be described as managing a portfolio of lower risk, highly recognized properties chosen to generate income and so on. If portfolio classification were required, the characteristics detailed above would be used to categorize the portfolio. For example, a heavily leveraged portfolio of undeveloped properties in third-tier real estate markets would be classified as opportunistic.
