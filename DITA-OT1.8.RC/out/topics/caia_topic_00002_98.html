LO 31.10

Operational risk refers to the uncertainty of economic outcomes resulting from imperfect implementation of a fund’s strategy. An omega-score predicts future operational risk using the fund’s age, size, fees, volatility, and historical performance. Funds with high operational risk also tend to have high financial risk and high failure rates.

  • A comprehensive due diligence process for a single fund may take anywhere from 75 to 100 hours and may cost in excess of $50,000, but it will likely lead to alpha for the investor.
