Arbitrage-free models differ from equilibrium models in that the models do not allow for arbitrage opportunities. Also, the currently observed term structure is used to determine the parameters of the model. This means the theoretically derived term structure is consistent with the observed term structure.

The Ho and Lee model was the first arbitrage-free model, and it states that next period’s short-term rate () is equal to the current short-term rate (rt) plus two adjustments. The first adjustment (denoted ) is a time-dependent adjustment that ensures the model fits with the current term structure. The second adjustment (denoted t+1) is the constant standard deviation of changes in the short-term rate. The Ho and Lee model is criticized because it allows for negative rates.
