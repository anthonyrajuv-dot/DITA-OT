Analyze: Location, Lot/Site, Building, and Financial Feasibility

  1. ANALYSIS
    1. Analyze: Location, lot/site, and building
      1. As with any business venture, buy-fix-sell involves risk.
        1. Risk cannot be eliminated.
        2. You can understand and analyze the project and, as a result, lower your risk.
      2. Your analysis will eventually become an integral part of your renovation plan.
      3. The key elements of the analysis include the following:
        1. Location
        2. Lot/site
        3. Building/structure
      4. Location
        1. Consider the property location and answer yes or no.
          1. If no, remove the property from consideration.
          2. If yes, move on to the next step.
        2. Ask yourself: Is it a good idea to sell a renovated property in the
          1. city?
          2. area?
          3. neighborhood?
          4. immediate surroundings (block or two radius)?
        3. Consider the adjacent properties.
        4. Once you have determined that the location is acceptable, turn your attention to the lot/site.
      5. Lot/site
      6. Building/structure
    2. Financial feasibility
      1. The key elements of a financial analysis include the following:
        1. Determining after-renovation value
        2. Acquisition costs
        3. Scope of work
        4. Construction costs
        5. Development costs (buying, owning, and selling costs)
        6. Profit targets
          1. Approximately 10 to 20 percent
          2. Flat rate: $10,000 to $20,000 per house
        7. Licensing issues
      2. Projecting profit, acquisition price, and construction budget
    3. After-renovation value (ARV)
      1. What will the property sell for upon completion?
      2. Competitive market analysis
        1. MLS—solds
        2. Classified advertisements
        3. Internet resources
      3. After renovation, your property will be very marketable compared to other unrenovated properties on the market.
        1. Clean
        2. Fresh paint and finishes
        3. Vacant—easy to show
      4. The market, not your costs, will determine the price.
