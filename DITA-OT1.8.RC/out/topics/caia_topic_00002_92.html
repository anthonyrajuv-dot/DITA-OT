LO 31.4

A fund’s investment style, the target markets a manager invests in, and the type of securities a manager invests in should be documented in order to fully understand the manager’s investment strategy.

A short volatility strategy involves taking short positions in call or put options so that rising volatility in the price of the underlying security exposes the investor to the risk that the options could be exercised against the investor.

Passive benchmarks are difficult to use for evaluating fund performance because of the following:

  • Passive benchmarks are ineffective for measuring the level of a manager’s skill.
  • Hedge fund strategies such as a long/short strategy cannot be captured by a long-only benchmark.
  • Concentrated portfolios look much different than broad-based security benchmarks.

A fund style index uses the aggregate performance of a group of funds that match the style of target fund as a benchmark.

Hurdle rates are appropriate measures of performance for absolute return funds whose returns do not relate to the performance of any one sector or market.

The process used for idea generation is a key component of a fund’s competitive advantage and is reflective of the fund manager’s skill.

A portfolio snapshot lets a potential investor see exactly what positions a manager is currently holding in the fund portfolio and gives insights into the manager’s strategy.

Capacity refers to the amount of assets under management a fund manager can handle and still implement the fund’s investment strategy effectively.
