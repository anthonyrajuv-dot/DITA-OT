Exam Focus

This topic review provides an overview of a formalized approach to manager selection. A key theme includes past performance and future performance. Past performance is not always indicative of future performance. Review processes and due diligence must be performed to understand if a manager has outperformed a relevant index and if she will or will not be able to continue outperforming. In general, top-quartile managers do not remain top-quartile managers as performance reverts to the mean over time. Rigorous investment and operational due diligence can help an investor understand when a fund will suffer in terms of performance. Also, candidates should understand the factors that influence the decision to terminate a manager.
