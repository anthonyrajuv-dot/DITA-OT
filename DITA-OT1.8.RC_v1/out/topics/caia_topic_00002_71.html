New developments in CDOs use different forms of collateral in a CDO structure to create exposure to different asset classes and cash flow patterns:

  • Distressed debt CDOs use distressed debt as the primary collateral component and can provide a series of tranches that have an investment grade credit rating even though the underlying collateral has a speculative grade.
  • Hedge fund CDOs, which are also called collateralized fund obligations (CFOs), are backed by hedge funds of various strategies.
