LO 8.3

Key measures that can be used to measure the performance of a private equity fund include the internal rate of return (IRR), the interim internal rate of return (IIRR), the modified internal rate of return (MIRR), the total value to paid-in (TVPI) ratio, the distribution to paid-in ratio (DPI), the residual value to paid-in (RVPI) ratio, and the public market equivalent (PME).

  • The IRR is the discount rate that equates the present value of cash inflows with the present value of cash outflows.
  • The IIRR is a rough estimate of IRR performance before fund liquidation.
  • The MIRR is designed to adjust the IRR measure for different reinvestment rate assumptions.
  • The TVPI measures the total value to the investor relative to the investment.
  • The DPI measures the cumulative investment returned relative to the invested capital.
  • The RVPI measures how much of the investorsâ€™ invested capital is still tied up in the fund.
  • The PME modifies private equity returns so that they can be directly compared to public equity market returns.

The J-curve (as well as the cash flow J-curve and the NAV J-curve) represents the typical successful private equity fund and shows declines in the early years followed by positive returns in later years.
