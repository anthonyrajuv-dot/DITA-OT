LO 10.1

Operational risk is defined as a series of risks (financial, reputational, etc.) that do not relate purely to investments. Operational due diligence (ODD), which is one of the two main areas of due diligence conducted by an investor, represents the process of evaluating this risk. The other main area, called investment due diligence (IDD), is focused on the investment strategies of the fund manager. Due to the relatively long investment horizons of private equity, it becomes very critical for potential investors to evaluate operational risks due to the increased likelihood and impact of risk exposure.
