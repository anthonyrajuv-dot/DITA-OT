LO 34.4

Each Asian country has its own regulations, making it much more difficult for U.S. hedge funds to get started throughout Asia compared to the EU.

  • In Hong Kong, the Securities and Futures Commission (SFC) regulates hedge funds and requires managers to have sufficient investment experience and meet capital requirements.
  • In Singapore, hedge funds are regulated by the Monetary Authority of Singapore (MAS) and must be an accredited investor or meet certain requirements to be licensed by MAS.
  • In South Korea, the Financial Supervisory Service (FSS) regulates hedge funds, which is operated by the Financial Services Commission (FSC). Current regulations are relatively strict compared to U.S. and European standards.

In Japan, the Securities and Exchange Surveillance Commission (SESC) regulates hedge funds. Japanese institutions can only invest in Japan-registered funds. Offshore funds cannot be marketed directly to Japanese investors.
