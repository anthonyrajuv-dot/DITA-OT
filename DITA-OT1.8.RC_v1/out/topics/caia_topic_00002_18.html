LO 18.3

  • Traditional merger arbitrageurs use leverage to buy stock of the target firm and short sell stock in the acquiring firm.
  • Regulatory risk refers to the uncertainty regarding the decisions made by regulators who may not approve a merger.
  • Financing risk refers to the uncertainty regarding the ability of the acquiring firm to obtain funding needed to consummate a merger.

The Sharpe ratio for merger arbitrage funds was very high as a result of moderately high returns and very low volatility. Similar to other event-driven strategies, the return distribution was negatively skewed and leptokurtic. Event-driven funds have consistently positive correlations with global equities, U.S. high-yield bonds, and commodities; and negative correlations with credit spreads and equity volatility.
