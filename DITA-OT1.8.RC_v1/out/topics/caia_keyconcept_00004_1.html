From 2005 to 2014, 5th and 25th percentile managers had positive excess returns on net-fee basis relevant benchmarks in nearly all individual years and longer investment periods. The 5th percentile managers outperform in every comparison, but the performance of top-quartile managers is more volatile. The volatility suggests that even if top-quartile managers have periods of outperformance, they frequently regress to the mean over time. Median manager performance is almost always below the index, across both asset classes and time.

Performance persistence tables indicate whether manager performance persists over time. Top-quartile managers generally do not continue to perform in the top quartile in subsequent periods.

This supports the maxim that past performance is not a good indicator of future performance. However, most investors still choose managers based on past performance.
