LO 6.1

Informational market efficiency refers to the speed and extent to which security prices adjust to new market information.

The three forms of market efficiency include weak form (security prices fully reflect all available security data on past prices and volumes), semistrong form (security prices fully reflect all publicly available information) and strong form (security prices fully reflect all publicly and privately available information).

Six factors that result in improved informational market efficiency are: (1) larger asset values, (2) higher frequency of trades, (3) little or no trading frictions, (4) low levels of regulatory constraints, (5) easier access to quality information, and (6) lower levels of uncertainty about asset valuation.
