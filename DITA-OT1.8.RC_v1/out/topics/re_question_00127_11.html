LOS 45.l: Compare effective convexities of callable, putable, and straight bonds.

CFA® Program Curriculum, Volume 5, page 351

Straight bonds have positive effective convexity: the increase in the value of an option-free bond is higher when rates fall than the decrease in value when rates increase by an equal amount. When rates are high, callable bonds are unlikely to be called and will exhibit positive convexity. When the underlying call option is near the money, its effective convexity turns negative; the upside potential of the bond’s price is limited due to the call (while the downside is not protected). Putable bonds exhibit positive convexity throughout.
