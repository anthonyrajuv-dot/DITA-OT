Examples of global structured products include:

  • U.S.-based structured product with multiple kinks = underlying asset + bear put spread − out-of-the-money call
  • French-based structured product with floors = underlying portfolio + portfolio of bear spreads + portfolio of short binary puts
  • German-based structured product with leverage = underlying asset + bull spread
  • U.K.-based absolute-return structured product = at-the-money up-and-out call + at-the-money down-and-out put
  • Swiss-based structured product based on absolute returns = at-the-money call + 2 at-the-money down-and-out puts − at-the-money put
  • Japan-based structured product based on multiple currencies: Investor makes a fixed interest rate payment in one currency in exchange for receiving a payment based on a fixed interest rate in different currency. Payment amount is usually limited with caps and floors.

Liquid structured products are listed on major market exchanges. A disadvantage is that listed structured products may fail to meet an investor’s particular needs due to the products’ standardized nature. However, these products offer investors better monitoring and improved access.
