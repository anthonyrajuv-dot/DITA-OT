The rebalancing yield describes the component of portfolio return attributable to rebalancing indexed commodity investments. Returns arise due to the assumption that commodity prices are mean reverting. Returns are greatest when portfolio assets are volatile with low correlations.

If mean reversion does not occur, rebalancing will not affect the expected future values of a portfolio, but will alter volatility. A decline in volatility will increase a portfolioâ€™s geometric return, but will not impact the expected future value of the portfolio.

Historically, commodity futures-based investments have provided positive returns and diversification benefits to portfolios.

Financialization of commodity returns is the result of the transition of commodity markets from exclusively commercial markets to markets with strong investor presence. This calls into question the predictive value of historical returns, especially given the observation that correlations between equities and commodities rose over time.
