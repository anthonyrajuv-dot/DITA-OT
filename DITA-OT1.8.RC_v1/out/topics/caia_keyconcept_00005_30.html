Agency relationships are important with regard to international real estate because: (1) the agent market is inefficient, so superior managers exist at a given fee level; (2) unlike traditional investments, other investors may not be monitoring managers; and (3) the real estate market is inefficient, so superior managers can generate persistent above-average risk-adjusted returns.

International real estate securities are often illiquid and possess large idiosyncratic risks and high fees and commissions. Investors may set minimum reservation prices, setting a price floor when selling an asset.

International real estate investments can include political, economic, legal, and exchange-rate risks. Exchange-rate risk factors in asset volatility in local currency and exchange-rate volatility and can be approximated as follows:

asset value × (1 + r + fx)
