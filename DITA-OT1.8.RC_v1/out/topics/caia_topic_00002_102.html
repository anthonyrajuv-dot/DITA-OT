Portable alpha involves exploiting opportunities to increase alpha while simultaneously managing beta exposure to a target level. This may be done with derivatives contracts such as futures, options, or swaps.

The futures position needed to hedge is equal to the size of the position to be hedged times the beta of the position being hedged relative to the index, which is:

futures contract’s notional value for hedging = value of the position to be hedged × beta

Implementing a portable alpha program is complicated because it requires:

  • Estimation of alpha.
  • Estimation of beta.
  • Construction of an appropriate hedge.

If alpha and beta are estimated incorrectly or are unstable across time, the actual results of a portable alpha strategy may be quite different from the expected outcome.
