LO 19.5

Infrastructure fund strategies include:

  • Active management, where investors have significant influence over financing, strategic, and operational issues. Active managers hold majority stakes in a small number of companies.
  • Passive management, where investors lack the desire or expertise to have outright control over companies. Passive managers hold minority stakes in a large number of companies.
  • Asset maturity, which can be classified as greenfield or brownfield infrastructure investments.
  • Geographic scope, which could include a regional focus or global focus.
  • Sector scope, which could range from social infrastructure to water and transportation sectors. Sector specialist funds could also specialize in public-private partnerships (PPPs) or private finance initiatives (PFIs) in the UK.
  • Core and peripheral infrastructure, which includes a broad range investment criteria.
