LO 15.10

From 2000 to 2014, equity REITs and mortgage REITs had higher returns than U.S. high yield bonds, world equities, commodities, and global bonds. However, global bonds had a higher Sharpe ratio than REITs due to the relatively high standard deviation of REITs. Furthermore, the correlations between equity REITs or mortgage REITs and the other four asset classes indicate that diversification can be achieved by having REITs in investors’ portfolios. In this same period, the NAREIT index had the highest return when compared with the NPI, the NCREIF TBI, and Moody’s RCA CPPI.
