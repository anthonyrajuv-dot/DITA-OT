LO 29.11

Investors in distressed debt need to be familiar with the financial metrics of the distressed entity and need to make projections for downside probabilities.

Liquidity risk is a typical concern for distressed investing, since securities may trade at wide bid-ask spreads. Reduced liquidity may cause large swings in price volatility and mark-to-market values.

Investors need to be familiar with international bankruptcy laws and regulations, and judges’ preferences and decisions, since a familiarity with the history of the judge’s ruling can help anticipate the outcome.
