The objectives of asset owners must be stated in terms of risk and return. While it is true that all investors would want to earn a higher return, an asset owner’s risk tolerance will dictate the amount of return possible in the market.

The most common way of representing an asset owner’s choice between risk and return is expected utility. A utility function is used represent an investment’s outcome to the investor’s utility. Risk aversion is when an investor has a concave utility function. This means that in order for an investor to take more risk, there must be a higher expected return.
