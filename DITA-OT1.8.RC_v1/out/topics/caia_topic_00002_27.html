  • Market efficiency tests are joint tests determining the validity of the market model and the existence of market anomalies. Thus, it is difficult to know if abnormal returns are the result of model misspecification or market inefficiencies.
  • Management of firms have incentives to meet market expectations, and studies find that when a firm reports net income significantly higher than operating cash flows, future negative earnings surprises are more likely to occur.
  • Hedge fund managers establish positions based on changes in a firm’s total accruals where total accruals = CA –CL –Cash + STDEBT – D&A.
  • Price momentum refers to the tendency of a higher expected price when there is an upward trend in prices and a lower expected price when there is a downward trend in prices.
  • Earnings momentum suggests that changes in earnings are positively correlated over time. When actual earnings exceed (are below) expected earnings, in the results is a positive (negative) surprise. One method used to measure earnings surprises is standardized unexpected earnings (SUE) = (EPS – ACEPS) / SDEM.
  • Equity hedge fund managers establish long positions in firms with share buyback programs and short positions in firms issuing large amounts of new stock.
  • Legal insider trading involves basing trades on the trading actions of senior executives.
