LO 17.1

Types of unlisted real estate funds include:

  • Open-end real estate funds are typically liquid investments with indefinite lives that allow investments and redemptions at any time. They generally have a lock-up period. They include property unit trusts (PUTs) and property authorized investment funds (PAIFs).
  • Closed-end real estate funds are less liquid investments that issue a limited number of shares to investors that can be traded and redeemed directly on the secondary market. In contrast, closed-end real estate mutual funds (CEMFs) are exchange-traded mutual funds whose shares are traded on organized exchange.
  • Real estate funds of funds invest in other real estate funds.

Four potential advantages of unlisted real estate funds include diversification, access to skilled managers, access to subsectors, and tax advantages. Potential disadvantages of unlisted real estate funds include cash drag, substantial fees, and leverage.
