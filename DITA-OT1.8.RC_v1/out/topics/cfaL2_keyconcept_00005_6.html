LOS 48.b

Because futures accounts are marked to market daily, the value after the margin deposit has been adjusted for the day’s gains and losses in contract value is always zero. The futures price at any point in time is the price that makes the value of a new contract equal to zero.

The futures price is a no-arbitrage price. For a future of term T on an asset with no storage costs or expected cash flows over the term of the contract, the futures price is:

FP = S0 × (1 + Rf)T
