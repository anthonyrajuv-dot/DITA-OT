Inventory Accounting

The choice of inventory cost flow method (known as the cost flow assumption under U.S. GAAP and cost formula under IFRS) affects the firm’s income statement, balance sheet, and many financial ratios. Additionally, the cost flow method can affect the firm’s income taxes and, thus, the firm’s cash flow.

Recall from Level I that cost of goods sold (COGS) is related to the beginning balance of inventory, purchases, and the ending balance of inventory.

COGS = beginning inventory + purchases – ending inventory

This can be rewritten as:

ending inventory = beginning inventory + purchases – COGS

Notice that the COGS and ending inventory are inversely related. In other words, if a particular valuation method increases the value of ending inventory, the COGS would be lower under that method.
