SchweserNotes™ Reference

Book 2, Pages 467–522

Global market participants continue to embrace the opportunities and meet the challenges arising from the growth and development of structured products such as interest rate derivatives, asset-backed securities, and insurance-linked securities. Know the various interest rates derivatives such as interest rate caps and floors, swaps, and callable bonds. Understand the characteristics and differences of various asset-backed securities. Insurance-linked securities, such as catastrophe (cat) bonds were created to transfer the risks of natural disasters and pandemics from insurance companies and reinsurers to investors. Understand the advantages and disadvantages of insurance-linked products to both investors and parties such as insurers and pension funds. Also, be able to distinguish between the different types of mezzanine finance. Know the mezzanine debt options that provide equity exposure to investors.
