LO 13.1

An investment in natural resources covers a spectrum, with one end representing a pure play on the resource and the other end representing investments in firms whose operations tie in some way to natural resources.

The three primary factors that drive the correlations between commodity prices and the returns of related firms are:

  • Extent that an operating firm has exposure to the commodity or has hedged its revenues and expenses.
  • Price elasticity of the good’s demand.
  • Price elasticity of the good’s supply.

Evidence has shown that in the short-term and long-term, performance of commodity-related firms differs from the price performance of the related commodity. Share prices for these firms are driven by commodity price changes, equity market conditions, and internal operations.
