LO 14.2

Real estate assets are heterogeneous both among and within subcategories. For example, commercial properties with long-term leases have bond-like characteristics, while other properties that exhibit equity-like characteristics perform well in a strong economy and less well in a weaker economy. Investors can use this heterogeneity to improve portfolio diversification.

Some asset allocators use a top-down approach, while others use a bottom-up approach. Top-down asset allocation involves a big picture approach to the allocation decision, focusing on broad categories. Bottom-up asset allocation emphasizes the attractiveness of individual properties rather than allocating based on broad property characteristics.
