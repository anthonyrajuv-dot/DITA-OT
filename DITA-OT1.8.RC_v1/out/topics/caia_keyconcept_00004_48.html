Governments typically hold foreign currencies in a central bank reserve account. The reserve account is affected by inflows and outflows from international trade and investment. The relationship can be stated as:

change in reserve account = change in current account + change in capital account

A countryâ€™s currency will tend to appreciate if the country has low inflation, high real interest rates, policies that attract capital, slow income growth that holds down demand for imports, and a comparative advantage in production of exports.

Commodity exports have unpredictable effects on the reserve account because commodity prices can be highly volatile. Countries with commodity-dependent economies have established stabilization funds to smooth the impact of commodity prices.
