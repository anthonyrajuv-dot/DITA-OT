LO 36.3

Longevity risk is the risk that a person or group of people will live longer than expected. Mortality risk is the risk that a person (or group) will die sooner than expected.

Longevity swap contracts are used to hedge longevity risk. A longevity swap contract entails a pension fund making fixed payments based on mortality assumptions in exchange for floating payments based on the pension plan’s actual mortalities (indemnity-based contract) or a mortality index (index-based contract).

Life insurance settlements (life settlements) consist of selling a life insurance policy to a third party who then assumes the premiums and becomes the beneficiary of the policy.

Viatical settlements are life settlements where expected mortality is less than two years. A viatical settlement is a transaction in which a policyholder, usually sick, sells his life insurance policy at a discount to face value.

The benefits of life/viatical settlements from the investor’s point of view are:

  • Low correlation with traditional assets.
  • Solid historical performance.
  • Only moderate drawdowns during the 2007–2008 financial crisis.

The risks of life/viatical settlements from the investor’s point of view are:

  • They can be affected by changes in interest rates, similar to bonds.
  • Performance data is scarce.
  • Life settlements are illiquid investments.
  • Legal risks.
  • Indices to measure performance and allow investors to hedge risk do not exist.
  • In general, risks facing life settlement investors include longevity risk, credit risk, policy availability risk, operational risk, regulatory risk, and tax legislation risk.
