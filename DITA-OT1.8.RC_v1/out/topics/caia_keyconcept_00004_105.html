LO 13.1

Private equity is structurally illiquid in that it has inherent illiquidity due to its long ex ante lockup provision. This differs from cyclical illiquidity, which occurs due to an information asymmetry between buyers and sellers.

Modeling PE cash flows offers four advantages:

  • Increasing the profit generated via overcommitments.
  • Improving investment returns for the portion of capital that is undrawn.
  • Calculating an economic value assuming a specific discount rate.
  • Monitoring the risk-return profiles and cash flows for a portfolio of funds.

The two forms of illiquidity risk for a private equity investor are funding risk (an inability to meet capital calls over time) and exit risk (an inability to liquidate an investment at a specific time or price).
