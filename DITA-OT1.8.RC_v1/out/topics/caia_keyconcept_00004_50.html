LO 5.3

There are four primary reasons for the establishment of a sovereign wealth fund:

  1. Insulate a sovereign budget from inherent volatility.
  2. Enable a central bank to offset excess liquidity.
  3. Accrue benefits for future generations.
  4. Invest in domestic infrastructure and economic growth initiatives.

Stabilization funds invest primarily in cash and fixed-income instruments. Savings, reserve, and development funds allocate a substantial amount to equity. Pension reserve funds have large allocations to alternative assets.

Dutch disease occurs when an inflow of foreign currency damages the long-run health of a domestic economy by increasing labor costs and appreciating the domestic currency. Sterilization to offset these effects may be carried out by the central bank selling bonds in the domestic currency or by the government directing excess currency inflows into a sovereign wealth fund.

A stabilization fund should estimate the size it needs to be for reserve adequacy, investing that amount in cash and cash-equivalent fixed income. Amounts in excess of the reserve adequacy level may be invested with a total return objective.
