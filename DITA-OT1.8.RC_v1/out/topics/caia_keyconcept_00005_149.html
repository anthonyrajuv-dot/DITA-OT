LO 35.5

To create an asset-backed security (ABS), an entity such as a bank creates a special purpose vehicle (SPV) to which it sells the respective assets like mortgages or automobile loans. Financial securities are issued from the SPV. Tranches, typically A, B, and C, are created to distinguish risks. Tranche A is usually the largest and safest with the highest credit rating and the lowest risk. It is supported by junior tranches B and C, which have lower credit ratings, higher risk, and higher expected yields. If a credit event occurs, tranche C experiences losses first, then tranche B, and finally tranche A.
