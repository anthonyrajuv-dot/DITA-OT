LO 4.2

Three approaches to risk measurement for a defined benefit plan are:

  • Asset-focused risk management: considers only the risk of plan assets.
  • Asset-liability risk management: focuses on the surplus of plan assets over liabilities.
  • Integrated asset-liability risk management: integrates management of the surplus with the operations of the plan sponsor.

Four key factors affect the risk of plan liabilities:

  • Interest rates.
  • Inflation.
  • Retirement cycle.
  • Mortality.

A plan sponsor needs to consider five key factors in addressing its risk tolerance:

  • Funding status of the plan.
  • Size of the fund.
  • Expected future contributions relative to the sponsor’s cash flow trends.
  • Sponsor’s general financial position as measured by the debt-to-equity ratio.
  • Participant demographics.

Using an asset-liability framework, plan sponsors can segregate plan assets into a safer “hedging bucket” and a more aggressive “growth bucket.”
