LO 8.5

Private equity performance can be measured against both listed private indices and public equity indices.

The public market equivalent (PME) is the index return measure that corresponds to the cash-weighted return that would have been earned by the private equity cash flows had they been invested in a public market index. The PME is positive (negative) when the public market has outperformed (underperformed) the private equity fund.

The PME ratio is also used to calculate fund performance relative to the public market. A PME ratio above 1.0 indicates that the fund outperformed the public market.
