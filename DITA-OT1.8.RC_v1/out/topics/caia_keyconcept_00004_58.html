LO 6.5

Because a family office is a taxable entity, investment strategies should be tax efficient and evaluated based on after-tax returns.

In the United States and many other jurisdictions, short-term gains are taxed at a higher rate than long-term gains. Section 1256 contracts, which include futures contracts, are taxed with 60% of gains at the long-term rate and 40% at the short-term rate. This makes futures a more attractive investment vehicle for family offices than other derivatives that provide the same pretax outcomes.

If hedge funds are used by the family office, tax efficiency can be improved by looking for a tax-efficient manager that uses long-term strategies and Section 1256 contracts.
