LO 1.3

Alternative investments can be described by several interconnected structures. Identifying influential structures provides a framework for analyzing traditional and alternative investments.

  • Regulatory structures include government regulation and taxation.
  • Securities structures include methods of cash flow securitization, which creates tradable units linked to an asset.
  • Trading structures include the development and execution of trading strategies utilized by investment managers and the resulting performance impact of the strategies.
  • Compensation structures include organizational and compensation arrangements that determine an investment manager’s fees, exposure to the investment’s performance, and possible conflicts of interest with investors.
  • Institutional structures include financial institutions and markets that affect the ownership and trading of a particular investment.
