LOS 16.a

Inventory cost flow methods:

  • FIFO: The cost of the first item purchased is the cost of the first item sold. Ending inventory is based on the cost of the most recent purchases, thereby approximating current cost.
  • LIFO: The cost of the last item purchased is the cost of the first item sold. Ending inventory is based on the cost of the earliest items purchased. Assuming inflation, ending inventory is smaller and COGS is larger compared to those calculated using FIFO. Higher COGS results in lower taxes and, thus, higher cash flow. LIFO is prohibited under IFRS.
  • Weighted average cost: COGS and inventory values are between their FIFO and LIFO values.
