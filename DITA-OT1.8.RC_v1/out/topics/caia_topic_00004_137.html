Eight Core Elements of the Operational Due Diligence Process

LO 10.2: Demonstrate knowledge of the eight core elements of the operational due diligence process.

For example:

  • List and discuss the eight steps investors follow when performing the ODD process on alternative investments.
  • Recognize considerations involved in executing the primary steps of the ODD process.

Figure 2 illustrates the primary steps associated with the alternative investment ODD process. The first seven steps are performed before making the investment and the eighth step is performed once the investment is made. Steps 1 through 4, as well as Step 8, are covered in detail in this chapter. Steps 5 through 7 are applicable universally and are covered in detail in the hedge fund area of the curriculum.

Although the steps are listed in an order that is often carried out sequentially, investors may find it more efficient to conduct several steps in parallel or even in a different order than presented here.

  1. Document collection
  1. Document analysis
  1. On-site visit
  1. Service provider review and confirmation
  1. Investigative due diligence
  1. Process documentation
  1. Operational decision
  1. Ongoing monitoring (if the investment is made)

Any of the steps listed may be revisited after the initial review process is completed. It is inevitable that follow-up and requests for additional documentation will occur.
