LO 29.7

Empirical models do not attempt to model default and do not try to directly estimate credit spreads or default probabilities. Instead, they estimate a credit score, which is a measure of the relative riskiness of firms or securities.

Altman’s Z-score model uses financial statement components and the market value of the firm’s equity by looking at liquidity, profitability, leverage, solvency, and activity. The model uses these five factors to generate a Z-score (credit score):

Z = 1.2 × X1 + 1.4 × X2 + 3.3 × X3 + 0.6 × X4 + 1.0 × X5

The five determinants of Altman’s Z-score are (1) X1: Working Capital/Total Assets, (2) X2: Retained Earnings/Total Assets, (3) X3: Earnings Before Interest and Taxes/Total Assets, (4) X4: Market Value of Equity/Book Value of Total Liabilities, and (5) X5: Sales/Total Assets.
