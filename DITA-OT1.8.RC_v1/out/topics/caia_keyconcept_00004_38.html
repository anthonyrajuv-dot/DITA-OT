Intergenerational equity refers to balancing the current spending needs (based on percentage of assets) with maintaining sufficient funds to support future spending needs. Endowment managers should consider consistent long-term spending rates irrespective of period returns to obtain long-term appreciation in the portfolio. Endowments and foundations need to generate returns far in excess of inflation to maintain the capital base and provide ongoing grants on an inflation-adjusted basis. For a foundation with no ongoing contributions, its minimum return target is 5% plus inflation in order to exist in perpetuity with no loss in the real value of the portfolio.
