The value of a swap to the fixed-rate receiver at a point in time is the difference between the present value of the hypothetical fixed-rate bond and the present value of the hypothetical floating-rate bond at the appropriate rates for each payment. The PV of the floating bond is the discounted value of the next floating payment (which is known) and the notional principal on that date.

Value to pay fixed side = PV (floating) − PV (fixed) Value to pay floating side = PV (fixed) − PV (floating)
