LO 17.7

  • Managed futures and macro funds had close to normally distributed returns with skewness near zero. They typically have higher standard deviations than most hedge funds but smaller maximum drawdown than equity markets.
  • A conceptual rationale for excess returns in managed futures is that CTAs play a valuable role as speculators who provide a service to natural hedgers.
  • The six risks associated with managed futures include: transparency risk, model risk, capacity risk, liquidity risk, regulatory risk, and lack of trends risk.
