The Five Steps of the Asset Allocation Process

LO 1.2: Demonstrate knowledge of the five steps of the asset allocation process.

For example:

  • Describe the five typical steps that must be taken to implement a systematic asset allocation program.

A systematic asset allocation approach is used for the benefit of the asset owners and results in the creation of an investment strategy related to their objectives and constraints. The process involves the following steps:

Step 1: Determine the asset owner and identify their objectives and constraints. These objectives and constraints can pertain to meeting future liabilities, as is the case for defined benefit pension plans, or other needs, such as the philanthropic activities of foundations.

Step 2: Specify the asset allocation approach. The investment policy statement provides an understanding of how the portfolio will be managed. Important parts of the IPS include objectives and constraints, the different asset classes that will be considered for investment, the choice of active or passive management, and when and how the allocation can be amended.

Alternative investment selection typically involves choosing managers, and the due diligence required in the process may reveal the best manager for the alternative investment is not possible. Therefore, this step must include some flexibility.

Step 4: Allocate capital. This step involves allocating the capital to the necessary asset class weightings.

Step 5: Monitor and evaluate investments. The changes in the market that were not predicted along with the underperformance or overperformance of managers ultimately will lead to differences between the expected and realized returns of the portfolio. The IPS allows for revisions to the allocation of assets.
