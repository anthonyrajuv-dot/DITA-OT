A comparison of infrastructure assets and other asset classes:

  • Infrastructure versus bonds: Both have steady cash flow streams that are subject to interest rate risk from rising interest rates. However, infrastructure typically hedges interest rate risk.
  • Infrastructure versus real estate: Both have stable cash flows, although cash flow variability is generally greater in infrastructure assets. Publicly traded, pure play infrastructure assets may be undervalued relative to public real estate assets.
  • Infrastructure versus buyouts: Investments in infrastructure assets tend to be longer term (20 years or more) and do not seek to make significant management and structural changes to invested companies.
  • Infrastructure versus equities: Infrastructure assets are less sensitive to business cycle fluctuations than equities. There is low correlation between the two asset classes.
