Documenting Operational Due Diligence Processes

LO 33.6: Demonstrate knowledge of documenting the ODD process.

For example:

  • Discuss structure and content of the documentation of results and conclusions of an ODD investigation.

Process documentation refers to the creation of a document that details the conclusions and results of an ODD investigation. Best practice is to create a document with an executive summary and a section that provides a detailed analysis of strengths of the fund and the firm. Other areas of the report should detail areas of operational risk. This documentation can facilitate an ongoing monitoring process, whether or not an investment is made in the current period or considered for an investment in a future period.
