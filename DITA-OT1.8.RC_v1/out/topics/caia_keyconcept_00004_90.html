LO 10.4

The structure of a private equity firm includes a general partner, an intermediary-level manager or investment adviser, the fund itself, limited partners, and the portfolio companies that receive the investments from the private equity fund.

The offering memorandum (OM), also known as the private placement memorandum (PPM), is the controlling legal document for the private equity fund. Fees (particularly management and performance) are a critical component of the OM/PPM. Investors may employ side letters in order to tailor certain provisions of the OM for that specific investor.
