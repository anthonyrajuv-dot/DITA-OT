The objective of the payoff-distribution approach is to create a portfolio with a return distribution that matches the benchmark. In contrast, the objective of the factor-based approach is to create a portfolio where the returns match a benchmarkâ€™s returns each period.

The payoff-distribution methodology used has its foundations in the classic option pricing model. The following equation illustrates how the desired distribution is constructed.

Pr(RHF < x) = FHF(x)

Assume the targeted distribution will be replicated using a reserve asset that is highly liquid. The monthly return on the reserve asset is Rt,R, and the idea is to obtain a derivative, G(Rr), written on the reserve asset so as to have its monthly return distribution be equal to the target probability distribution as follows:

Pr(RHF < x) = Pr(G(Rr) < x)

Empirical studies suggest the payoff-distribution approach may be successful in matching higher moments for a few strategies, but the mean returns are always lower.
