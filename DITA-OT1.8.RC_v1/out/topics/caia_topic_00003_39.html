Structured Products

Topics 6.1–6.4

Weight on Exam

10–15%

SchweserNotes™ Reference

Book 2, Pages 184–278

Structured products are becoming increasingly prevalent in institutional portfolios. Candidates should be familiar with credit risk and credit risk models (structural and reduced-form). Credit derivatives are also extensively covered and candidates should be well versed on the intricacies of credit default swaps and other credit derivatives. Candidates should also understand the structure, application, and differences between the types of CDOs. In addition, understand equity-linked structured products and their embedded exotic options as well as the motivations for both investors and issuer. Finally, understand the motivations of both investors and issuers with regard to structured products.
