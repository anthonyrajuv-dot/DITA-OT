LO 18.2

An activist investment strategy involves:

  • Identifying corporations that do not maximize shareholder wealth.
  • Investing in positions that benefit from changes in corporate governance.
  • Executing favorable corporate governance changes.

The following categories distinguish between different types of shareholder activists:

  • Financial vs. social activists.
  • Activists vs. pacifists.
  • Initiators vs. followers.
  • Friendly vs. hostile.
  • Active vs. passive.

The key players in financial activism are active initiators, active followers, and passive followers.

Agency costs are all costs associated with conflicts of interest between shareholders and management.

Three common agendas for activists are related to:

  1. Management compensation and interlocking board of directors.
  2. Capital structure and dividend policy.
  3. Mergers and divestitures.

The historical returns of activist funds are very high with moderately high volatility.
