Exam Focus

A family office is a customized structure for managing the wealth of ultra-high net worth families. Key points to understand here include the variety of investment objectives and governance structures a family office can have to meet each family’s specific needs; the differences between the goals of the founding generation and the goals of its successors; and how spending rates, tax treatments, lifestyle assets, and philanthropic efforts can influence the management of a family’s portfolio. Be able to list the advantages of a family office as compared to outside solutions for private wealth management.
