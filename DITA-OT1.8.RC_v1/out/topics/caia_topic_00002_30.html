  • Short sales are orders to sell securities that the seller does not own. The short seller sells high and buys low, and profit is generated when the short security declines.
  • The biggest distinction between short-bias hedge funds and long/short hedge funds is that short-bias hedge funds maintain a net short exposure to market risk.

  • Equity long/short strategies typically have a net long position.
  • Long/short funds and the equity hedge fund index have similar mean returns, volatilities, and Sharpe ratios.
  • Market-neutral hedge funds attempt to create mean neutrality, or zero beta exposure to market, risk and, therefore, do not move with the market.
  • Low volatility and low correlation with equities and extreme market events are the most attractive attributes of market-neutral strategies. However, the low volatility implies large allocations to market-neutral funds are required to gain diversification benefits.
