Asset allocation refers to two concepts: (1) the steps taken in determining the proper asset classes for an investorâ€™s assets by the asset manager, and (2) the breakdown of the chosen portfolio asset classes.

Security selection builds on the asset allocation by selecting the investments within the asset classes chosen.

In a concentrated portfolio, as opposed to diversified, of only cash and a single stock, the performance is going to be determined mostly by security selection. If the portfolio is well diversified, consisting of thousands of domestic and international stocks, security selection has a minimal impact on performance.
