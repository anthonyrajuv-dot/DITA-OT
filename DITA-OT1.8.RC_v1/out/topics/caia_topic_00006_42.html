SchweserNotes™ Reference

Book 2, Pages 214–466

The readings contained within this topic provide a broad overview of the managed futures and hedge fund industries and the trading strategies and risk management tools used by traders. For managed futures, be able to describe the characteristics and functions of business structures, regulatory frameworks, and key individuals. Understand the process of structuring investments in managed futures and the difference between commodity trading advisers and commodity pool operators. For hedge funds, understand the execution steps and risks of relative value strategies, directional strategies, and credit strategies. Be familiar with how volatility can be measured, managed, and traded. In addition, be able to explain the types, benefits, and drawbacks of hedge fund replication products. Finally, understand the importance of operational due diligence and global regulation with regard to alternative investments.
