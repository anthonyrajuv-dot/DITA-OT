LO 23.6

Equity-based strategies—hedge a commodity firm’s equity against commodity price risk from commodity production or consumption. Components include:

  • Commodity rights—the value of commodity resources that have yet to be extracted.
  • Enterprise value—the residual value of the firm’s assets.

If the enterprise value is undervalued (overvalued), the trader buys (sells) the firm’s equity and sells (buys) commodity futures to hedge commodity rights exposure.

Debt-based strategies—use derivatives to hedge a commodity firm’s bond default risk associated with commodity exposure. Typically, default exposure is hedged with options (puts for producers, calls for consumers). However, the hedge may also be executed using futures contracts.
