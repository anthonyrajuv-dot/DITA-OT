LO 29.3

The Merton model is a structural model that views capital structures with options, and uses the assumptions of the Black-Scholes formula for valuing option values. The model can be used to estimate both call and put option values, as well as credit spreads.

The Merton model is useful as starting point before considering more complex models. However, model parameters may not be readily observable, including the return volatility and the asset market values, and the model also does not explain well the credit spread on short-term securities.

The Merton model has four important properties (1) sensitivity to maturity, (2) sensitivity to asset volatility, (3) sensitivity to leverage, and (4) sensitivity to the risk-free rate.
