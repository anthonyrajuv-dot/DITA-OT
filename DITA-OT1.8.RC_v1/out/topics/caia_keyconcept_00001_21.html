If returns are uncorrelated over time, then the variance of a multiperiod log return equals the sum of the inter-period log-return variances. In addition, if the variances of daily log returns are identical (homoskedastic), then the T-period log-return variance equals T multiplied by the variance of the daily log return. This also implies that the T-period log-return standard deviation equals the square root of T multiplied by the standard deviation of the daily log return.
