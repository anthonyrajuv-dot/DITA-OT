LO 6.3

The first generation that establishes a family office may have wealth concentrated in a company the founder built or managed. The portfolio should be managed to diversify the family’s wealth with bonds and equities that are uncorrelated with the founder’s company and industry.

The first generation typically is most concerned with preserving the wealth it has built. A conservative absolute return objective is appropriate, with a benchmark return of a fixed percentage above short-term riskless interest rates.

Subsequent generations have greater needs for tax management and wealth generation. Portfolio investments typically shift toward high allocations to private equity and other illiquid asset categories.
