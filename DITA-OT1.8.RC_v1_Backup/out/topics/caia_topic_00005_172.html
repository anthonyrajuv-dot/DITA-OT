Distinguishing Private Equity and Hedge Fund Operational Due Diligence

LO 33.1: Demonstrate knowledge of the differences in the operational due diligence (ODD) processes for hedge fund investment and private equity investment.

For example:

  • Describe the major differences between hedge funds and private equity that affect the ODD processes for those types of investments.

Hedge funds and private equity funds have substantial differences that affect ODD processes:

  • Hedge funds trade their positions more frequently.
  • Hedge funds have holdings that are more liquid.

However, various hedge fund strategies hold positions that are illiquid and are considered private equity hybrids, so be aware that there is a continuum of strategies between hedge funds and private equity. Investors need to tailor their ODD processes based on where a fund lies on this continuum, as the valuation and operational procedures will vary across strategies.
