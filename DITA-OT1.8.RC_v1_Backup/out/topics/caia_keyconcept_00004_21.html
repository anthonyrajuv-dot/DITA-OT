LO LRTM.1

In a buy-in transaction, a pension plan pays an up-front premium to an insurer in exchange for periodic benefit payments (sponsor maintains assets and liabilities). In a buy-out transaction, a pension plan sends an up-front premium as well as its assets and liabilities to an insurer.

In a longevity swap transaction (longevity insurance), a pension plan pays periodic premiums to a counterparty (sponsor maintains assets and liabilities). The plan sponsor receives periodic payments that are based on the difference between expected and actual benefit payments.
