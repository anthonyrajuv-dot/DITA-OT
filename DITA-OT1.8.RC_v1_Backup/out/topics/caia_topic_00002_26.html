LO 20.1

  • Equity hedge funds establish long positions in undervalued equities and short positions in overvalued equities. The mix of the long/short positions determines the amount of systematic risk exposure. Common strategies include equity long/short funds, equity market-neutral funds, and short-bias funds.
  • Hedge funds earn excess returns by playing an important role as market makers in providing liquidity.
  • Equity hedge fund managers trade on temporary inefficiencies that often include short positions. These managers face less competition for finding overvalued stocks. Asynchronous trading and market overreactions and underreactions are other examples of information inefficiencies where hedge fund managers are rewarded with complexity premiums.
  • Hedge fund managers may speculate by taking on abnormal risk in exchange for abnormal returns.
