Traditional private equity secondary strategies include direct purchases of limited partnership interests from a seller or participating in a private equity secondaries fund. Nontraditional strategies include preferred capital and portfolio debt financing.

Preferred capital refers to co-investing in a limited partnerâ€™s portfolio on preferred terms, receiving a larger share of distributions until the investor has recovered its investment plus a preferred return, and then receiving a smaller share of distributions.

Portfolio debt financing is lending to a limited partner. Interest and return of principal are owed regardless of portfolio distributions.

Among these strategies, debt financing has the lowest risk and lowest target return; traditional strategies have the highest risk and highest target return; and preferred equity strategies fall in the middle.
