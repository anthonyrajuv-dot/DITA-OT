LO 8.6

Peer groups are collections of similar private equity funds customizable for various investment styles and used for benchmarking. A peer-group cohort is a group of private equity funds/investments that shares similar characteristics such as size, industry focus, investment stage, and vintage year.

Databases used for peer-group benchmarking all have limitations that are important to understand. Various biases such as survivorship bias, selection bias, valuation bias, instant history bias, and stale pricing play a role in private equity benchmarking.

Both quantitative and non-quantitative methods may be used to control the risk differential between PE funds and benchmarks.
