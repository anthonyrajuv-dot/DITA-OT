LO 27.2

A balance sheet CDO is used by a financial institution such as a bank or insurance company to remove assets from its balance sheet. There are generally three goals of a balance sheet CDO:

  1. Reduce credit exposure to a certain client or industry.
  2. Capital infusion (i.e., improve liquidity).
  3. Reduce regulatory capital charges.

Arbitrage CDOs are issued primarily by money managers who are seeking to earn an arbitrage profit between the return on the collateral and the funding costs of the CDO. A money manager can profit from an arbitrage CDO in three ways:

  1. A transaction fee from selling portfolio.
  2. The CDO management fee.
  3. The return from holding the equity tranche.
