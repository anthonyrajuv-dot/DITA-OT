Analyze: Scope of Work

  1. SCOPE OF WORK
    1. Considerations
      1. Cost versus value of improvement.
      2. The most profitable rehab properties are often those requiring the least work (cosmetics).
      3. Your scope of work should have the end buyer in mind.
      4. Which items or amenities will be important to a buyer?
      5. Cost versus value study.
      6. Predict the items that the truth-in-sale/municipal inspection, buyer’s home inspection, or financing inspection will uncover, and solve those issues.
      7. The scope of work will eventually lead to a construction estimate and dictate construction costs.
      8. Scope of work
        1. Site characteristics
          1. Shape, configuration
          2. Topography, soils
          3. Access, visibility
          4. Drainage
            1. Positive drainage (away from structure)
            2. No ponding
          5. Vegetation—grass (or lack thereof)
          6. Flower and landscape beds—curb appeal items only
          7. Trees and shrubs
          8. Driveway and walks
          9. Porches and decks
          10. Steps and stoops—25 percent deteriorated
          11. Fences
        2. Garage
          1. Slab—25 percent deteriorated
          2. Structure
          3. Fire barrier
