Smart Beta

LO 32.1: Demonstrate knowledge of smart beta strategies.

For example:

  • Recognize the distinguishing characteristics of smart beta strategies from active alpha-based strategies.
  • Describe the objectives of smart beta strategies.
  • Discuss how smart beta strategies can be used in portfolio management.

Passive index strategies and strategies that pursue alpha are often viewed on opposite ends of the investment spectrum. In recent years, the hybrid strategy of smart beta has increased in prominence. Smart beta strategies use rules-based portfolio weights that differ from market capitalization weights in an attempt to deliver improved risk and returns. Often, smart beta strategies try to capture anomalies that have been identified in empirical research. For, example, a firm may construct a small-cap, value strategy with stocks that have low volatility and price momentum because of the empirical evidence of these effects. Smart beta strategies can be used for both traditional and alternative investments.

Characteristics of smart beta strategies include all of the following:

  • Attempt to capture a systemic risk premium.
  • Utilize rules that rely on security characteristics (e.g., small-capitalization).
  • Have portfolio weights that differ from market capitalization weights.
  • Maintain broad portfolios.
