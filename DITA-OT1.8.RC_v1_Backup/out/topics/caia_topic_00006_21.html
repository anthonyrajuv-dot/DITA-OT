SchweserNotes™ Reference

Book 1, Pages 301–426

Private equity is a fairly long topic and is largely qualitative. Understand how private equity acts as an intermediary, pooling capital and deploying the capital to potentially high-return investments. Understand the private equity environment and how information asymmetries in this environment require monitoring, nontraditional risk measures, and performance benchmarks. Be familiar with the general partner/limited partner relationship and understand why limited partners often use over-commitment strategies. Understand the secondary market for private equity. Know the measures used to calculate private equity returns and understand why private equity returns are subject to the J-curve effect. Also, understand how liquidity is managed in private equity funds.
