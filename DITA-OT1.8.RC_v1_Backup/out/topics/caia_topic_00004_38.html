Private Equity Secondary Market

LO PES.1: Demonstrate knowledge of the mechanics of the private equity secondary market.

For example:

  • Identify the three parties involved and their roles in secondary market transactions.
  • Describe the importance of the valuation (reference) date.
  • Explain the effects of distributions, capital calls, and changes in the NAV on the final payment.

A private equity secondary transaction is a way for a limited partner in a private equity fund to exit the investment early. Typically, this requires the approval of the general partner. The buyer of a limited partnership interest (i.e., secondary buyer) acquires all the sellerâ€™s rights and responsibilities, which include an open capital commitment.

The buyer and seller agree on a purchase price and a reference date (or valuation date) for the transaction. The price paid to settle the transaction is the purchase price adjusted for cash flows that occur after the reference date. The buyer will reimburse the seller for capital calls after the reference date. Distributions the seller receives after the reference date will decrease the settlement payment from the buyer. However, there is typically no adjustment for changes in the value of the limited partnership interest after the reference date. These are simply gains or losses to the buyer.
