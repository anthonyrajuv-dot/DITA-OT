A relative value strategy is any strategy trying to profit as a result of mispricings of multiple securities.

Derivatives pricing models assume that arbitrage opportunities are nonexistent. Transaction costs usually prohibit arbitrageurs from profiting from put-call parity violations.

Modern finance suggests that arbitrageurs help move stock prices to their fundamental values. Fundamental value is the value of a security taking into account all of the relevant economic information. Five factors limiting pure and/or risk arbitrage opportunities include (1) fundamental risk, (2) noise traders, (3) leverage risk, (4) market frictions, and (5) agency relationship.
