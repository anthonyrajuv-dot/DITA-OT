International real estate returns typically exhibit low correlations with traditional asset classes and, therefore, offer significant diversification benefits. Diversification can be measured through the efficient frontier. However, international real estate portfolios may shift the efficient frontier to the left, rather than shifting it up due to the difficulty in hedging currency risk.

The capital asset pricing model (CAPM) can be used to determine the optimal level of diversification. Investors should adjust the market weights to their target optimal weights.

As a rule, investors in high tax brackets should favor real estate investments that offer significant tax benefits.

Leverage is the use of debt financing in real estate transactions and magnifies losses and gains. Investors benefit from the tax deductibility of the loan, and they can generate a sizeable depreciation tax shield with a relatively small equity investment. However, leverage increases return volatility.
