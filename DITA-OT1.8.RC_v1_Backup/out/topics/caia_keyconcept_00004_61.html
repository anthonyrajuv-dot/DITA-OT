LO 6.8

As the benefactors of a family office are considering their estate planning needs, they need to decide if they want to be charitable or philanthropic. Charity involves giving to a cause to solve an immediate need but without accountability by the recipients. Philanthropy involves trying to solve an underlying problem on an ongoing basis.

A family office may consider impact investing to pursue social objectives of the founding family. Impact-first investing puts the desired social goal ahead of return requirements. Finance-first investing seeks to earn an investment return but also considers the social impact of the investments made.
