The Bayou Fund collapse was due to fraud perpetrated by Samuel Israel III and Daniel Marino. Israel and Marino falsified financial records, used commission rebates from a broker created by the firm (Bayou Securities) to hide losses, and created an accounting firm to serve as the independent auditor. Lessons learned from the Bayou fraud include:

  • Investors should conduct background checks on the principals of a fund.
  • Investors should demand hedge funds use a well-known, independent auditor.
  • Investors should realize that regulations are not a suitable substitute for due diligence.

Bernie Madoff claimed to use an option collar strategy but was actually operating a massive Ponzi scheme. Madoff created fictitious financial reports indicating impressive results but was merely paying redemptions out of capital from new investors. Lancer Group was an equity-based hedge fund that relied on using both private and public small-cap equity securities. Lancer achieved a stated high watermark of $1.2 billion in assets and $600 million in equity. However, in the aftermath of its implosion, the final valuation of the Lancer portfolio was $70 million. Lessons learned from the Lancer case include:

  • Funds with no transparency can be quite dangerous.
  • Be suspicious of internal valuations.
  • Beware of window dressing.
  • Ensure adequate audit and regulatory safeguards.
