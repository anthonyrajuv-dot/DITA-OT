The four-quadrant model looks at the equilibrium between the real estate and asset markets. The right side of the model represents the property market, while the left side represents the asset market. The NE quadrant measures rent as a function of demand and supply for real estate. The NW quadrant establishes a property price using rent and a given capitalization rate. The SW quadrant establishes the equilibrium point where new construction costs equal the property price. The SE quadrant measures changes in long-term supply given changes in construction costs.

Changes in interest rates and tax code legislation can impact the demand for real estate. A sudden increase in demand will lead to increased rents, higher asset prices, more robust new construction activity, and a greater supply of real estate.
