LO 19.7

Public-private partnerships (PPPs) represent a form of risk transfer from a government to the private sector. PPPs include water and waste management, transportation, hospitals, schools, and prisons.

The private investor in a PPP typically creates a special purpose vehicle and uses a combination of debt (from 60% to 90% of project cost) and equity financing. Investors provide equity capital flows to the SPV, which contracts a separate company to design and construct the asset. Assets generate return through user fees.

PPPs can be used for both greenfield and brownfield projects. PPPs transfer risk from the public sector to the private sectors, improving the quality, supply, and cost structure of infrastructure investments.

Criticisms of PPPs include reduced accountability, underinvestment, and poor asset maintenance.
