LO 21.5

  • Hedge fund liquid alternatives have lower minimum investments, lower fees, and greater transparency than privately placed hedge funds but are subject to more regulation and thus are less flexible for their managers.
  • Liquid alternatives are regulated by the UCITS Directive in Europe and the ’40 Act in the United States.
  • The UCITS Directive limits leverage to 200% of NAV; restricts investments in real assets, private equity, and commodities; and limits asset concentrations in single funds, issuers, and institutions.
  • The ’40 Act regulates liquid alternatives’ leverage and diversification and requires regular liquidity and symmetric performance fees.
  • The largest segments of hedge fund liquid alternatives are equity strategies and multialternatives. Liquid alternatives are also available for macro and managed futures strategies, but typically not for event-driven and relative value strategies.
  • Liquid alternatives typically have less risk and lower expected returns than private placements.
