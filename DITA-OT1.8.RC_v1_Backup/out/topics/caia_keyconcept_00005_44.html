Farmland investment returns are driven by land-generated cash flows and the individuals who are tasked with managing the farm. Macroeconomic factors also play a key role in U.S. farmland returns, with the most prominent being inflation, interest rates, industrial production, and the strength of the dollar. Several indices, including the NCREIF Farmland Index, measure farmland real estate performance. The price appreciation of farmland from 2009 to 2014 appears to have been due to the willingness of existing farmers to pay higher prices for available land.
