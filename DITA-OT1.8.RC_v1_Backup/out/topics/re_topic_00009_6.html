Analyze: Exterior and Interior

  1. EXTERIOR AND INTERIOR INSPECTIONS
    1. Inspection
      1. Exterior inspection—from the top to the bottom
        1. Chimney
        2. Roof
          1. Serious roof problems may show up as water stains in the attic, room ceilings, or cornice.
        3. Shingles
        4. Flashing
        5. Overhang
        6. Siding
        7. Windows and exterior door trim
        8. Windows and exterior doors
        9. Exterior foundation wall
        10. Top of foundation—six inches above grade
        11. Service wiring
  2. INTERIOR: FOUNDATION AND MECHANICALS
    1. Inspection
      1. Interior inspection—from the bottom to the top
        1. Basement floor slab
        2. Foundation walls
          1. Cracks—significance depends on characteristics
          2. Displaced walls
        3. Structural supports
          1. Posts, beams, joists
          2. Level and square
          3. Added support (screw jacks)—investigate
        4. Overall condition
        5. Mechanical systems
          1. Plumbing—includes water supply and distribution, waste and vent, and gas lines
            1. Municipal water source
            2. Gas lines
            3. Water heater
            4. Distribution piping
            5. Private well
              • Professional well inspection is required in some municipalities/states
              • Septic field is subject to municipal/state inspections
            6. Waste, drain, vent pipes
        6. Electrical
          1. Main panel—amperage
            1. Breakers
          2. Circuits
            1. Kitchen circuit
            2. Bath circuit
          3. Distribution wiring
          4. Outlets
          5. Other considerations
            1. Switched outlet or switched light per room
            2. Hardwired smoke detectors with battery backup
              • Hallway of each floor or level
              • Bedrooms (could be battery)
            3. Ground fault circuit interrupter (GFCI) outlets in laundry, kitchen, and bath
        7. Heating, ventilation, and cooling (HVAC)—type
          1. Forced air
          2. Gravity (octopus)
          3. Hot water
            1. With pump
            2. Without pump (gravity)
          4. Steam
          5. Geothermal system
          6. Condition
            1. Have the unit inspected and certified
            2. Age
            3. Supply air
            4. Scorching
            5. Rusting
            6. Clearances
            7. Too warm or hot to touch
            8. Holes
            9. Water storage tank leaking
          7. Tips and resources
            1. Home inspection classes are helpful
            2. Property inspection sample checklist
