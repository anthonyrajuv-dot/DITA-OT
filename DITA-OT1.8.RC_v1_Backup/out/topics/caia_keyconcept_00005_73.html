First-generation commodity indices generally have long-only front-month contracts into second-month contracts without regard to the shape of the term structure. Contangoed commodity markets result in a significant negative impact on returns.

Second-generation commodity indices seek to improve index performance through four roll techniques: (1) moving contracts to a further location on the forward curve and/or adjusting for market conditions, (2) implied roll technique, (3) enhanced roll techniques, and (4) constant expiration techniques.

Third-generation indices use more active contract and weighting selection than the previous generations of indices. Criteria that may be used to determine index composition include momentum, term structure, market-neutral positions, and rule-based approaches that incorporate technical and fundamental analysis.
