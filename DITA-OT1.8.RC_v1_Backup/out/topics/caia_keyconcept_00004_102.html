LO 12.3

For private equity investments, there is typically a difference between the current (estimated) market price and the intrinsic value (the present value of future cash flows). Changes in the net asset value of a private equity fund have little to no bearing on the cash flows paid or received by a limited partner, whose position is vulnerable to liquidity risk and decisions made by the fund manager. Overcommitments (and therefore commitment risk) are a concern when an investor is forced to meet a capital call and may be unable to generate the cash needed to meet the obligation.
